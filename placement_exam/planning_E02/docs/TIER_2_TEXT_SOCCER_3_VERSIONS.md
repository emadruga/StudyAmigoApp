## Como usar este documento

Cada versão abaixo é uma passagem independente de ~300 palavras sobre A Seleção Brasileira. As três versões cobrem ângulos diferentes do mesmo tema, para que o professor possa escolher a mais adequada ao momento pedagógico.

**Método de seleção de palavras para Tier 2**: o aluno lê o texto com Google Translate disponível. Toda palavra que ele precisou traduzir para entender a frase **e** que ele não teria produzido sozinho em inglês entra na lista candidata. As **20 palavras-chave** ao final de cada versão são o conjunto pré-designado para criação de flashcards.

**Formato de cartão recomendado (Upgrade 3 — Cloze)**:
> Frente: `Brazil has _______ in every World Cup since 1930.`
> Verso: `competed — competiu/participou. Colocação: compete in a tournament.`

## Versão 1 — História e Conquistas

### The Story of A Seleção

Brazil's national football team, known as A Seleção, is one of the most successful teams in sports history. The team played its first international game in 1914 against the English club Exeter City. Since then, Brazil has competed in every FIFA World Cup ever played — a record no other nation has matched.

The golden era of Brazilian football began at the 1958 World Cup in Sweden. Led by a young player named Pelé, Brazil defeated the host nation in the final and lifted the trophy for the first time. Four years later, they won again in Chile. The greatest achievement came in 1970, when Brazil dominated every opponent and won the tournament in Mexico. That squad featured legendary players like Pelé, Jairzinho, and Rivelino. Many experts consider it the best team ever assembled in the sport.

After a long wait, Brazil won the World Cup again in 1994 in the United States. The team then reached a new level of consistency: they won the Confederations Cup four times and claimed their fifth World Cup title in 2002 in South Korea and Japan. During that tournament, stars like Ronaldo, Ronaldinho, and Rivaldo carried the team to victory.

Brazil's rivalry with Argentina is one of football's most famous. The two South American giants have competed fiercely for decades, and every match between them attracts millions of fans around the world.

Today, Brazil is preparing for the 2026 FIFA World Cup in North America. With talented players like Vinícius Júnior and Rodrygo leading the attack, and a solid defensive line, the team aims to win its sixth title. The pressure is enormous, but for A Seleção, the World Cup is always the ultimate goal.

## Versão 2 — Os Jogadores

### The Stars of A Seleção

Brazil's national football team has always been home to exceptional players. Over the decades, A Seleção produced some of the greatest footballers in history. Players like Pelé, Garrincha, Ronaldo, and Ronaldinho wore the famous yellow jersey and changed the way people think about football.

Today, a new generation of stars represents Brazil on the world stage. Vinícius Júnior, known as Vini Jr., plays as a winger for Real Madrid and the national team. He is famous for his explosive pace, creative dribbling, and ability to score in important matches. In 2022, he scored the winning goal in the UEFA Champions League final. In 2024, he won the Best FIFA Men's Player award — one of the highest honors in world football.

Rodrygo is another key forward who plays alongside Vini Jr. at Real Madrid. Known for his composure under pressure, Rodrygo has the ability to score in crucial moments. He is comfortable playing across the entire front line, which makes him a versatile option for the national team.

Neymar is Brazil's all-time leading goal scorer with 79 goals, just ahead of the legendary Pelé with 77. Despite recent injuries, Neymar remains one of the most recognizable athletes in world sport.

In defense, Marquinhos serves as the team's captain. He plays for Paris Saint-Germain in France and is known for his intelligence, composure, and leadership on the pitch. Alisson Becker, the goalkeeper for Liverpool, is considered one of the best in his position in the world. He won the Best FIFA Goalkeeper award in 2019.

Together, these players bring skill, experience, and passion to every match. Brazil's fans expect nothing less than excellence from A Seleção.

## Versão 3 — Estilo de Jogo e Copa do Mundo 2026

### Brazil and the Beautiful Game

Few teams in football play with the creativity and style that Brazil is known for. The concept of "jogo bonito" — the beautiful game — is deeply connected to the Brazilian football tradition. It describes a way of playing that combines technical skill, improvisation, and attacking flair. When Brazil plays well, the game looks more like an art form than a sport.

This philosophy was most visible in 1970, when Brazil's squad demonstrated what jogo bonito truly means. The players moved the ball with speed and precision, and every goal seemed better than the last. That team is still remembered as one of the greatest in football history. Today, attackers like Vinícius Júnior and Rodrygo carry that tradition forward with their pace, creativity, and ability to finish under pressure.

The 2026 FIFA World Cup will be held across the United States, Canada, and Mexico. Brazil qualified for the tournament without difficulty and entered as one of the top favorites. The team is placed in Group C alongside Morocco, Scotland, and Haiti.

At the 2022 World Cup in Qatar, Brazil was considered one of the strongest teams. However, they were eliminated in the quarterfinals by Croatia. The match ended 1–1 after extra time, and Croatia won 4–2 in a penalty shootout. That defeat was painful for players and fans alike.

Now, the team is determined to go further in 2026. Head coach Carlo Ancelotti is known for managing elite clubs and developing talented players. With his experience and Brazil's depth of quality, expectations are high. Fans around the world believe this could finally be A Seleção's year to claim a sixth World Cup title and prove that the beautiful game still belongs to Brazil.

## Notas pedagógicas para o professor

### Qual versão usar?
| Versão | Melhor para... |
|--------|----------------|
| **V1 — História** | Alunos que já têm noção de tempos verbais passados; ênfase em sequência narrativa |
| **V2 — Jogadores** | Alunos mais engajados com futebol atual; vocabulário de posições e atributos |
| **V3 — Estilo/Copa 2026** | Alunos com maior nível de Tier 2; texto com mais vocabulário abstrato e colocações |

### Ativação antes da leitura (sugestão)
Perguntar aos alunos: *"Quem é o melhor jogador do Brasil hoje? Por quê?"* — isso ativa conhecimento prévio e reduz a carga cognitiva durante a leitura em inglês.

### Criação de cartões — instrução específica para Tier 2
1. Leia o texto uma vez completo com Google Translate disponível.
2. Sublinhe toda palavra ou expressão que você precisou traduzir para entender a frase.
3. Das palavras sublinhadas, escolha até **10** que você não conseguiria usar em uma frase em inglês sozinho.
4. Compare com a lista de palavras-chave acima — priorize as palavras que aparecem em ambas as listas.
5. Para cada palavra escolhida, crie um cartão no formato **cloze** usando uma frase do texto.
6. No verso do cartão, escreva a tradução + a colocação mais comum + **uma frase sua** (Nível 4: use a palavra numa frase sobre seu curso ou vida).
