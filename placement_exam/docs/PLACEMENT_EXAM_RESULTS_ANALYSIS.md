## Executive Summary

The placement exam was successfully administered with **57 students** participating—an excellent response rate. The three-path branching system worked as designed, with students self-selecting into Path A (17.5%), Path B (64.9%), and Path C (17.5%). However, the data reveals several critical findings that require immediate attention and calibration for future iterations.

### Key Findings

1. **✅ Path A worked as a confidence-builder**: All 10 Path A students scored between 7-10/10 (mean: 8.00), demonstrating that the cognate-heavy Band 1 questions successfully provided an accessible testing experience.

2. **⚠️ Major concern: 60% of Path A students are potential underestimators**: 6 out of 10 Path A students scored 8-10/10, suggesting they have more English knowledge than they self-reported. These students should be offered the full test or closely monitored during E01.

3. **⚠️ Q25 (Anchor-Hard) was too easy**: 80.9% of Path B/C students answered Q25 correctly, far exceeding the expected 15-30% threshold. This indicates Band 3 questions may not be challenging enough to distinguish true Tier 3 students.

4. **⚠️ Tier placement skew**: Based on test scores alone, 56.1% of students would be placed in Tier 1, 33.3% in Tier 2, and 0% in Tier 3. This suggests either:
   - The student population has lower baseline English than expected, OR
   - The test thresholds need recalibration, OR
   - Band 3 questions are not sufficiently difficult

5. **✅ Good path distribution**: The 17.5% / 64.9% / 17.5% split across paths A/B/C is close to the expected distribution and suggests the self-assessment gate worked well.

## 5. Question Difficulty Analysis (Selected Items)

| Band | Question | Correct % | Target % | Assessment |
|------|----------|-----------|----------|------------|
| Band 1 | What does "control" mean? | 100% | 70–90% | ✅ Appropriate (cognate) |
| Band 1 | The temperature _____ 25 degrees. | 70% | 70–90% | ✅ Appropriate |
| Band 1 | The computer _____ information quickly. | 20% | 70–90% | ⚠️ **Too hard for Band 1** |
| Band 2 | What is "achieve"? | 76.6% | 40–70% | ⚠️ Slightly too easy |
| Band 2 | She _____ the experiment last week. | 83% | 40–70% | ⚠️ Too easy for Band 2 |
| Band 2 | Steel is _____ than plastic. | 55.3% | 40–70% | ✅ Appropriate |
| Band 3 | Passive voice sentence | 44.7% | 10–40% | ⚠️ Too easy for Band 3 |
| Band 3 | If temperature _____ too high... | 44.7% | 10–40% | ⚠️ Too easy for Band 3 |

### Key Findings:

1. **Q10 ("The computer _____ information quickly") is too difficult for Band 1**:
   - Only 20% of Path A students answered correctly (expected: 70–90%).
   - This is a subject-verb agreement question requiring "-es" suffix recognition, which may be beyond the cognate-recognition level of Path A students.
   - **Recommendation**: Move Q10 to Band 2 or simplify the distractor options.

2. **Band 2 questions are generally too easy**:
   - Q11 ("achieve") and Q12 (past simple) both exceed 75% correctness, placing them closer to Band 1 difficulty.
   - This contributes to the overall low score problem—if Band 2 questions are easy but students still score low, it indicates a genuine low baseline or Band 3 dominance.

3. **Band 3 questions are not challenging enough**:
   - Passive voice and conditional questions were answered correctly by ~45% of Path B/C students, exceeding the 10–40% target.
   - This explains why Q25 (80.9% correct) performed so poorly as an anchor-hard.
   - **Recommendation**: Introduce more complex Band 3 questions involving:
     - Multi-clause sentences with embedded relative clauses
     - Advanced vocabulary (Academic Word List)
     - Inferential comprehension requiring synthesis across multiple sentences

## 7. Key Conclusions & Actionable Insights

### Conclusion 1: The Test Successfully Engaged Students But Needs Recalibration

**Evidence**:
- 57/57 students completed the test (100% completion rate).
- Path A students scored 7–10/10 (mean: 8.00), demonstrating that Band 1 was accessible and confidence-building.
- No students abandoned the test mid-way (all submissions are complete).

**Implication**: The three-path branching architecture achieved its primary goal of providing an anxiety-free experience for Path A students while collecting useful data. However, the scoring thresholds and question difficulty need adjustment.

---

### Conclusion 2: 60% of Path A Students Are Underestimators

**Evidence**:
- 6 out of 10 Path A students scored ≥8/10.
- These students selected "Nunca estudei inglês" but demonstrated strong cognate recognition and basic vocabulary.

**Implication**: Brazilian students' cultural tendency to say "eu não sei nada de inglês" is confirmed. Many Path A students have **passive English knowledge** from:
- Cognate recognition (Portuguese-English overlap)
- Media exposure (movies, music, games, YouTube)
- Technology interfaces (apps, software in English)

**Actionable Next Steps**:
1. **Immediately contact these 6 students** and offer them the option to take the full test (Bands 2 + 3).
2. If they decline, place them **provisionally in Tier 1** but flag them for close E01 monitoring.
3. If their E01 retention rate is ≥50% (medium performance), **move them to Tier 2**.
4. For **Version 2 of the exam**, consider adding a second screening question to Path A to better identify underestimators upfront.

---

### Conclusion 3: Band 3 Questions Are Not Challenging Enough

**Evidence**:
- Q25 (anchor-hard) was answered correctly by 80.9% of Path B/C students (expected: 15–30%).
- Other Band 3 questions (passive voice, conditionals) were answered correctly by ~45% (expected: 10–40%).
- **Zero students** scored in the Tier 3 range (19+/25).

**Implication**: The current Band 3 questions are functioning as **Band 2 questions**, not as Tier 3 differentiators. This means:
- We **cannot reliably identify Tier 3 students** with this test version.
- The Tier 3 threshold of 19/25 (76%) is unrealistic given the current question difficulty.

**Actionable Next Steps**:
1. **For this semester (Version 1.0)**:
   - **Lower the Tier 3 threshold** to 16/25 (64%) to identify students who scored at the top of the distribution.
   - Look for students who scored 12–14/25 **and** answered Q25 correctly—these are likely Tier 3 candidates despite not reaching 19/25.

2. **For Version 2.0 (next semester)**:
   - **Replace Q25** with a more challenging question (e.g., 6-sentence passage requiring cross-sentence inference).
   - **Add 2–3 more Band 3 questions** testing:
     - Complex relative clauses (e.g., reduced relative clauses: "The data collected during the experiment...")
     - Advanced academic vocabulary from the Academic Word List (e.g., "hypothesis," "methodology," "correlation")
     - Contrastive discourse markers (e.g., "Although X, Y; however, Z")

---

### Conclusion 4: The Student Population Baseline Is Lower Than Expected

**Evidence**:
- Mean score for Path B/C students: 9.66/25 (38.6%)
- 85% of Path B/C students scored in the Tier 1 range (≤10/25)
- Even Path C students ("took English courses") scored low (mean: ~10.5/25)

**Implication**: The assumption that "high school English" = Tier 2 baseline is **not accurate** for this population. This is consistent with Brazilian public education context, where:
- English classes are often minimal (1–2 hours/week)
- Focus is on grammar drills, not communicative competence
- Students have little opportunity for reading practice

**Actionable Next Steps**:
1. **Accept that Tier 1 will be the largest tier** (50–60% of students).
2. **Do not lower Band 2 difficulty** to artificially inflate Tier 2 numbers—this would compromise the validity of tier distinctions.
3. **Focus Tier 1 materials on high-frequency vocabulary and simple present/past** (consistent with the Foundation tier design in [READING_MATERIAL_METHODOLOGY.md](../../server/docs/READING_MATERIAL_METHODOLOGY.md)).
4. **Validate placements with E01 data** (Signal 2) before finalizing tier assignments.
