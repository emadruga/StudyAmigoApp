## 1. Purpose and Context

### 1.1 Background

Students are halfway through E01, a 3-week exercise that introduces grammar awareness through spaced repetition, focusing on **Verbal Tenses**. The deck contains **108 cards** and is reviewed daily through the JAVUMBO application using a modified SM-2 algorithm.

The placement exam (administered in February–March 2026) established a provisional tier assignment for each student based on placement test scores alone (Signal 1). Final tier assignments are due by **Week 3 (March 21, 2026)** and require a second signal (Signal 2) from E01 performance data.

### 1.2 Goals of the Prep Exam

This prep exam serves two simultaneous purposes:

1. **For students**: A self-assessment tool to measure how well they are retaining Verbal Tenses content midway through E01. It motivates reflection on their own SRS study habits.
2. **For the instructor**: Provides Signal 2 — a direct measurement of knowledge transfer from the SRS deck to an explicit recall task. Combined with in-app retention metrics, this finalizes tier placement.

### 1.3 Exam Type

This is a **voluntary, non-mandatory formative assessment**. Participation carries no penalty for absence and no direct grade impact. Its dual audience is:

- **Consistent students** (a minority): a concrete progress checkpoint and motivational reward for their sustained SRS effort.
- **Disengaged students**: a visible signal that falling behind has consequences — students who are not reviewing will likely score poorly, serving as an early warning before the semester grade is affected.

The exam is not a grade component in itself, but its results will inform the instructor's final tier placement (Signal 2) and can highlight students at risk before the end of E01.

## 3. Design Principles

The prep exam deliberately mirrors the structure of the placement exam to maintain consistency across assessment instruments.

| Dimension | Placement Exam | Mid-E01 Prep Exam |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Purpose** | Diagnose incoming proficiency level | Measure SRS retention of Verbal Tenses |
| **Tier 1 questions** | 10 (Band 1 only) | 10 (core tenses) |
| **Tier 2 questions** | 25 (all bands) | 20 (core + extended) |
| **Format** | Multiple choice, section branching | Same |
| **Instructions** | Bilingual PT/EN | Same |
| **Delivery** | Google Forms (programmatically generated) | Same |

### 3.1 Content Alignment with the E01 Deck

Questions must map directly to card content in the Verbal Tenses deck. The intent is to test **active recall of what the SRS system has been presenting** — not abstract grammar knowledge. Students who reviewed consistently should have an advantage over those who relied on prior knowledge alone.

### 3.2 Question Type Progression

- **Tier 1**: Recognition and completion tasks (lower cognitive demand). Students identify correct forms, match time adverbs, and complete sentences from multiple-choice options.
- **Tier 2**: Adds contrast, context selection, and a mini-passage with mixed tenses (higher cognitive demand).

All questions remain **multiple choice with 4 options** to stay consistent with the placement exam format and allow automatic grading via Google Forms quiz mode.

### 3.3 Question Construction Rules

These rules apply to every question bank generated for this exam series (prep exam, final exam, and any future variants). They were established through review of early bank versions and must be enforced when writing or reviewing questions.

**Rule 1 — Avoid 3rd person singular subjects in sentence-completion questions.**

Do not use `he`, `she`, or `it` as the grammatical subject of a gap-fill sentence unless the question is explicitly testing 3rd person singular morphology (e.g., the `-s` ending, `does`/`doesn't`, `Does...?`). These forms were not covered in the Verbal Tenses SRS deck during the 1st bimester and should not appear as the expected correct answer. Use `I`, `you`, `we`, or `they` instead.

> *Rationale*: Students cannot be tested on a form they were not exposed to. Using 3rd person singular as a distractor (wrong option) is acceptable, but it must never be the correct answer unless the tense being tested explicitly requires it.

**Rule 2 — Every sentence-completion question must include a time marker or structural anchor that makes exactly one option grammatically correct.**

A sentence with a bare subject and a verb gap (e.g., `"They _____ in New York."`) typically allows multiple valid tenses (`live`, `lived`, `will live`). This is not acceptable in a multiple-choice exam. Every completion question must include one of the following to constrain the answer space:

- A **time adverb or time expression** that unambiguously signals the tense:
  - Simple Present: `every day`, `every summer`, `always`, `usually`
  - Simple Past: `yesterday`, `last night`, `last week`, `last summer`
  - Present Continuous: `right now`, `at the moment`, `currently`
  - Simple Future: `tomorrow`, `next week`, `next month`, `soon` *(with care — see below)*
  - Present Perfect: `already`, `yet`, `ever`, `so far`, `twice`, `three times`
  - Past Continuous: `when + Simple Past clause` (e.g., `"when the alarm went off"`)

- A **structural anchor** in the stem that makes certain options grammatically impossible (e.g., an `-ing` form already present in the stem rules out all non-continuous auxiliaries).

- A **contextual scenario** that restricts the pragmatic reading (e.g., visible evidence for `going to`, spontaneous decision for `will`).

> *Note on "soon"*: `"They _____ here soon."` is ambiguous because `"They are here soon"` (Present Continuous with near-future meaning) is possible in colloquial English. Prefer explicit markers like `"tomorrow morning"` or `"next week"` when testing Simple Future.

**Rule 3 — Test only tenses and structures present in the SRS deck for the current bimester.**

Before writing questions, verify which tenses and grammatical structures actually appear in the students' Verbal Tenses flashcard deck for the bimester. Do not test any structure that was not reviewed via SRS, even if it is related to the topic.

For the **1st bimester**, confirmed absent from the deck:
- **"going to" future** — the SRS deck covers Simple Future exclusively with `will`. No flashcard uses the `be going to` construction. Do not include "going to" as a correct answer, a distractor, or in any scenario-based question.

> *How to verify*: Query the student database directly:
> ```bash
> sqlite3 ~/.cache/studyamigo/YYYYMMDD/user_dbs/user_NNN.db \
>   "SELECT flds FROM notes ORDER BY id;" | grep -i "going to"
> ```
> If the query returns no results, the structure is absent from the deck.

**Rule 4 — No question text may repeat a sentence from a previous bank in this exam series.**

Each bank file must be checked against all prior banks before finalisation:
- `placement_exam/bases/question_bank.json`
- `exam_prep/bases/prep_exam_bank.json`
- Any previously distributed final exam bank

The `question_text` field is the comparison key. Paraphrasing the same sentence with minor lexical changes (e.g., swapping one noun) is acceptable only if the structural focus is clearly different.

## 4. Question Count and Distribution

### 4.1 Tier 1 — 10 Questions (Foundation)

All students answer these questions. Focus: core tenses only, recognition-level.

| # | Tense | Skill Tested | Example Format |
|---|-------|-------------|----------------|
| 1 | Simple Present — affirmative | Correct verb form (3rd person) | Sentence completion, 4 options |
| 2 | Simple Present — negative | Correct auxiliary (don't/doesn't) | Sentence completion |
| 3 | Simple Present — interrogative | Correct word order (Do/Does) | Identify correct sentence |
| 4 | Simple Past — regular verb (affirmative) | -ed formation | Gap fill, 4 options |
| 5 | Simple Past — regular verb (identification) | Recognize the tense in context | "Which tense is used here?" |
| 6 | Present Continuous — affirmative | is/are + verb-ing | Sentence completion |
| 7 | Present Continuous — negative | is/are + not + verb-ing | Sentence completion |
| 8 | Present Continuous — identification | Recognize tense in context | "Which tense is used here?" |
| 9 | Future with *will* — affirmative | will + base verb | Sentence completion |
| 10 | Time adverb matching | Connect adverbs to tenses | Match: yesterday / now / tomorrow → tense |

**Estimated time**: ~15 minutes

### 4.2 Tier 2 — 20 Questions Total (Developing)

Tier 2 students answer all 10 Tier 1 questions plus 10 additional questions below.

| # | Tense/Skill | Type |
|---|-------------|------|
| 11 | Past Continuous — formation | Gap fill (was/were + verb-ing) |
| 12 | Past Continuous — context usage | Sentence completion in a scenario |
| 13 | Past Continuous vs. Simple Past — contrast | Choose the correct sentence from 4 options |
| 14 | Present Perfect — formation | have/has + past participle, gap fill |
| 15 | Present Perfect — context usage | When to use Present Perfect vs. Simple Past |
| 16 | Present Perfect vs. Simple Past — contrast | Error correction / choose correct |
| 17 | Future with *going to* — affirmative | am/is/are + going to + verb, gap fill |
| 18 | Future with *going to* — negative | not going to, sentence completion |
| 19 | *Will* vs. *going to* — context choice | Scenario-based selection (decision vs. plan) |
| 20 | Mixed tenses — mini-passage | 3–4 sentence passage, identify tense of each underlined verb |

**Estimated time**: ~25 minutes

## 5. Google Form Structure

Identical branching technique to the placement exam (`create_placement_form.py`):

```
Section 1: Bilingual Instructions (PT/EN)
           + Estimated time (15 min Tier 1 / 25 min Tier 2)

Section 2: Core Tenses — Q1–10 (ALL students)
           → Routing question at end:
             "Are you in Tier 2 (students with prior English training)?"
             → Yes  → go to Section 3
             → No   → Submit

Section 3: Extended Tenses — Q11–20 (Tier 2 only)
           → Submit
```

### 5.1 Form Settings

| Setting | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Quiz mode | Enabled (automatic grading) |
| Collect email addresses | Yes (for roster join by email) |
| Limit to 1 response | Yes (prevent duplicates — lesson learned from placement exam) |
| Accept responses | Must be toggled ON manually after API creation |
| Mandatory | **No** — participation is optional |

### 5.2 Scoring

| Group | Max Score |
|-------|-----------|
| Tier 1 | 10 points |
| Tier 2 | 20 points |

Each question is worth 1 point. No partial credit.

## 6. Assessing SRS and Memorization Effectiveness

The prep exam score alone is not the full picture. Its value is maximized when **cross-referenced with each student's SRS data** from the JAVUMBO activity monitor.

### 6.1 The 2×2 Diagnostic Matrix

| SRS Retention (app data) | Prep Exam Score | Interpretation | Action |
|--------------------------|-----------------|----------------|--------|
| High | High | ✅ SRS is working — deep transfer to explicit recall | Confirm current tier; on track |
| High | Low | ⚠️ Rote recognition without active recall | Check if student is skimming cards; discuss study habits |
| Low | High | ℹ️ Prior knowledge, not SRS-driven | Student may not be reviewing consistently; still on tier track but monitor effort |
| Low | Low | 🚨 Neither SRS nor prior knowledge is helping | Immediate intervention; consider meeting individually |

### 6.2 SRS Metrics to Pull per Student (Before Grading)

From the JAVUMBO activity monitor, extract for each student at the halfway point:

- **Retention rate**: % of cards answered Good or Easy — proxy for learning quality
- **Cards reviewed (volume)**: total number of reviews — proxy for consistency
- **Average review time**: seconds per card — proxy for engagement (too fast = not reading; too slow = struggling)
- **Ease distribution**: % Again / Hard / Good / Easy — reveals struggle patterns

### 6.3 Relationship to Signal 2 for Final Tier Placement

The three-signal placement matrix (from `PLAN_FOR_PLACEMENT_EXAM_v1.2.md`, Section 6.3) requires:

- **Signal 0**: Self-assessment path (A/B/C) — already collected
- **Signal 1**: Placement test score — already collected
- **Signal 2**: E01 performance — **this exam + SRS data combined**

For Signal 2, use the following combined score:

```
Signal_2 = 0.60 × (SRS retention rate) + 0.40 × (prep exam score, normalized 0–1)
```

This weighting reflects that SRS consistency over 3 weeks is a stronger signal than a single exam snapshot.

## 7. Flagged Students (Underestimators)

The 5–6 students who selected Path A ("never studied English") but scored 8–10/10 on the Band 1 placement test should receive the **Tier 2 version** of this prep exam (20 questions).

Their performance here, combined with their SRS data, is the definitive Signal 2 needed to resolve their provisional placement before March 21.

| Expected Outcome | Decision |
|-----------------|----------|
| High prep exam score AND high SRS retention | Move to Tier 2 |
| High prep exam score BUT low SRS retention | Move to Tier 2, but flag for engagement monitoring |
| Low on both | Confirm Tier 1 |
